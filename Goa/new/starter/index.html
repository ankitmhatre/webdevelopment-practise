Explore Goa at ease

North Goa

Beaches like Baga, Calangute, Candolim are most famous tourist attactions in north Goa, these beaches are well known for water sports and other beachside activities. There are some secluded beaches in north Goa like Ashwem, Mandrem & Arambol which are just half hour away from calangute.

South Goa

South Goa is mainly know for its peaceful beaches, hilly spots & water falls. It has many beautiful small villages covered by coconut & cashew trees.

Nightlife in Goa

Goa nightlife livens up with dizzy lights, great music, delicious goan food that makes for the perfect ambience you'd wish for. While the nightlife in Goa undoubtedly beats that of the other states of the country, that's not all of what it entails. Goa is a perfect infusion of varieties, and it quite literally has something for everyone to do at night.

Goan Food

Since Goa is located on the west coast of India, along the shore of the Arabian Sea, Goan cuisine is dominated by spices and flavours. The staple food of Goa is rice and fish curry. Most of the dishes incorporate coconuts, rice, fish, pork, meat and local spices like kokum. The cuisine of Goa is mostly dominated by seafood which includes shark, tuna, pomfret, and mackerel fish.

Goan Culture

The Gaon Culture is a combination of Indian and Portugese culture. The long Portuguese rule has influenced the culture in more ways than one and many of the Portuguese traditions are reflected in the Goan culture even today.
